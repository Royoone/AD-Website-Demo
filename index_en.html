Welcome to Atopic Dermatitis Staphylococcus Repository

This repository is dedicated to collecting, organizing, and analyzing Staphylococcus strains related to atopic dermatitis, providing comprehensive data support and analytical tools for research on the relationship between atopic dermatitis and the skin microbiome.

The repository integrates strain data from global research institutions, including clinical samples, healthy controls, and environmental isolates, aiming to promote collaboration and innovation in skin microbiome and skin disease research.

Research Highlights

Microbial Phylogenetic Diversity

Demonstrates the phylogenetic relationships and genome sequencing quality of different bacterial phyla (including Firmicutes, Bacteroidetes, Actinobacteria, etc.), providing important reference data for microbiome research. Phylogenetic tree analysis helps us understand the evolutionary relationships and diversity among different bacterial phyla.

Learn More

Pathogen-Host Immune Interactions

Comparative analysis of standard Staphylococcus aureus strains and specific strains with keratinocytes reveals differences in immune responses and their underlying mechanisms. Research shows that AD strains induce Th2-type immune responses, while standard strains mainly activate Th1-type immune responses.

Learn More

Pathogen Adhesion Molecular Mechanisms

Details the molecular interaction network between Staphylococcus aureus and human keratinocytes, including specific binding between bacterial surface proteins (such as Protein A, ClfA, etc.) and host receptors. These interactions collectively promote bacterial adhesion, internalization, and inflammatory responses on the skin surface.
